DialogEval: A Benchmark for Cross-Framework Annotation of LLMs

Unpacking the "Black Box" of Classroom Dialogue Analysis

ACL 2026 Submission Anonymous Authors
Paper Code & Data

Abstract

Automated Classroom Dialogue Encoding (ACDE) is critical for pedagogical improvement, yet LLMs' ability to grasp deep structures remains under-explored. We propose DialogEval, a benchmark utilizing the "Three Bs" framework to evaluate decoding capabilities Between, Behind, and Beyond the words. We evaluated eight LLMs across FIAC, IRF, and SEDA frameworks. Results identify a Logic Threshold for contextual dependency and reveal that domain-specific models exhibit Domain Rigidity and Proactive Inhibition, where prior schemas interfere with novel frameworks.

The "Three Bs" Theoretical Framework

We operationalize ACDE into three cognitive dimensions, establishing an evolutionary gradient of cognitive demands.

1. Between The Words

Contextual Logic (IRF)

Focuses on structural causal linking. Requires the model to resolve contextual dependencies (e.g., distinguishing closed Evaluation from open Feedback).

2. Behind The Words

Pedagogical Intent (FIAC)

Requires decoding latent pedagogical intentions. We investigate how instructional density affects intent inference (Rule-based vs. Logic-driven strategies).

3. Beyond The Words

Sociocultural Norms (SEDA)

The highest inference level. Examines how pre-existing domain knowledge and geocultural backgrounds reshape the interpretation of sociocultural norms.

Methodology & Setup

  • Sliding Window Context: A context-aware mechanism ($u_{t-2}...u_{t+2}$) to capture local context without "lost-in-the-middle" issues.
  • Hierarchical Prompting: Four levels from Vanilla (Zero-shot) to Chain-of-Thought (Reasoning Scaffold).
  • Diverse Model Zoo: Tested 8 LLMs including Reasoning-enhanced (DeepSeek-R1), General (Qwen, GPT-4o), and Domain-specific (EduChat) models.

Capillary Analysis: Lexical Triggers

We define Relative Error Risk (RER) to identify "lure words" that trigger cognitive biases. $$RER(w,c)=\frac{P(Error|w\in u_{t},label=\epsilon}{P(Error|label=c)}$$

Hallucinated Interactivity

Domain-specific models (e.g., EduChat-R1) systematically misidentify silence as initiation.

Trigger: "Here", "Now"
Result: Silence -> Student Initiation
Cause: Proactive Inhibition

Semantic Anchoring Pitfall

Models anchor on surface markers instead of logical sequences.

Trigger: "Isn't it?" (Interrogative)
Result: Feedback -> Feedback + Initiation
Cause: Failure to cross Logic Threshold
